ROBOTICS AND EMBEDDED SYSTEMS Robots and connected embedded digital systems provide smart solutions that extend human capabilities. Powered by artificial intelligence and data generated in real-time by nature, people and the systems within robots and drones, the degrees of autonomy surrounding us increase as they perform valued tasks. UATâ€™s Robotics and Embedded Systems degree provides students with the foundation for the design, implementation and analysis of systems that make robots, drones and other platforms viable tools. A degree in Robotics and Embedded Systems incorporates mechanical design, digital logic design, embedded programming, machine vision, sensing, big data and artificial intelligence into developing and designing robotic systems that solve useful problems. The Classification of Instructional Programs (CIP) code for Robotics and Embedded Systems is 15.0405.

ROBOTICS AND EMBEDDED SYSTEMS DEGREE OBJECTIVES

1. Design and complete robotic and embedded systems solutions that address real-world situations and challenges.

2. Demonstrate embedded microprocessor system and circuit skills.

3. Develop mechanical control systems by implementing transducers, actuators, feedback, vision and sensing systems, and other mechanical systems into robotic platforms.

4. Examine and assess a variety of applications within the field of robotics.

5. Model, analyze, and design systems or processes that integrate hardware and software to control autonomous mechanical systems.

6. Implement artificial intelligence and data systems into robotic platforms.
